# Headline Formulas

Proven patterns for headlines that get clicks without being clickbait.

---

## The Golden Rule

**Your article must deliver on the headline's promise.**

Clickbait overpromises and underdelivers. Good headlines promise AND deliver.

---

## High-Performing Formulas

### Numbers and Lists

Numbers are specific. Specific is compelling.

- **[Number] Ways to [Achieve Result]**
  - "7 Ways to Write Better Headlines"
  - "21 Productivity Tips That Actually Work"

- **[Number] [Things] You Need to [Action]**
  - "5 Tools You Need to Start a Podcast"
  - "10 Books You Need to Read This Year"

- **[Number] Mistakes [Audience] Make**
  - "9 Mistakes New Bloggers Make"
  - "12 Mistakes That Kill Your SEO"

- **[Number] [Things] Every [Audience] Should Know**
  - "15 Things Every Developer Should Know"
  - "8 Things Every First-Time Manager Should Know"

**Tip:** Odd numbers often outperform even numbers. Don't ask why.

### How-To

The classic instructional headline. Clear and direct.

- **How to [Achieve Result]**
  - "How to Start a Blog"
  - "How to Write a Headline"

- **How to [Achieve Result] in [Timeframe]**
  - "How to Learn Python in 30 Days"
  - "How to Double Your Traffic in 3 Months"

- **How to [Achieve Result] Without [Obstacle]**
  - "How to Get Fit Without a Gym"
  - "How to Build a Website Without Coding"

- **How to [Achieve Result] (Even If [Challenge])**
  - "How to Write a Book (Even If You Have No Time)"
  - "How to Start Investing (Even If You're Broke)"

### Questions

Questions create curiosity gaps. Readers click to find the answer.

- **Why [Something Unexpected]?**
  - "Why Do Most Startups Fail?"
  - "Why Isn't Your Blog Growing?"

- **What If [Provocative Scenario]?**
  - "What If Everything You Know About SEO Is Wrong?"
  - "What If You Could Work 4 Hours a Day?"

- **Are You Making These [Topic] Mistakes?**
  - "Are You Making These Email Marketing Mistakes?"
  - "Are You Making These Common Grammar Mistakes?"

- **[Question Your Audience Asks]?**
  - "Should You Learn React or Vue?"
  - "Is WordPress Still Relevant in 2024?"

### The Guide/Ultimate/Complete

Position your content as the definitive resource.

- **The Complete Guide to [Topic]**
  - "The Complete Guide to Content Marketing"
  - "The Complete Guide to Home Brewing"

- **The Ultimate [Topic] Guide**
  - "The Ultimate SEO Guide for Beginners"
  - "The Ultimate Guide to Remote Work"

- **[Topic]: Everything You Need to Know**
  - "Markdown: Everything You Need to Know"
  - "Retirement Planning: Everything You Need to Know"

**Use sparingly.** Only when your content truly is comprehensive.

### Results/Transformation

Promise a specific outcome.

- **How I [Achieved Result]**
  - "How I Grew My Newsletter to 10,000 Subscribers"
  - "How I Quit My Job and Became a Freelancer"

- **How [Person/Company] [Achieved Result]**
  - "How Basecamp Built a $100M Business"
  - "How She Paid Off $80K in Debt in 2 Years"

- **[X] to [Y]: How to [Transform]**
  - "Zero to Hero: How to Learn Web Development"
  - "Beginner to Pro: How to Master Photography"

### Comparison/Versus

Help readers make decisions.

- **[Option A] vs [Option B]: Which Is Better?**
  - "React vs Vue: Which Is Better for Your Project?"
  - "Mailchimp vs ConvertKit: Which Is Better for Creators?"

- **[Option A] vs [Option B]: [Specific Comparison]**
  - "WordPress vs Ghost: Speed, Features, and Pricing Compared"

### Secrets/Hidden/Little-Known

Imply insider knowledge (but actually deliver value).

- **[Number] Little-Known Ways to [Result]**
  - "7 Little-Known Ways to Improve Your Writing"
  
- **The Secret to [Desirable Outcome]**
  - "The Secret to Writing Viral Headlines"

- **What [Experts] Don't Tell You About [Topic]**
  - "What SEO Experts Don't Tell You About Backlinks"

**Caution:** Don't overuse. Can feel manipulative if content doesn't reveal anything new.

### Negative/Warning

Fear of loss is powerful. Use responsibly.

- **Stop [Bad Practice]**
  - "Stop Using These Outdated SEO Tactics"
  - "Stop Apologizing in Your Emails"

- **[Number] Things You Should Never [Do]**
  - "5 Things You Should Never Put in Your Resume"

- **Why [Common Practice] Is Killing Your [Metric]**
  - "Why Posting Daily Is Killing Your Engagement"

- **The [Topic] Mistake That's Costing You [Loss]**
  - "The Design Mistake That's Costing You Sales"

## Power Words

Words that add emotional punch:

**Urgency:** Now, Today, Quick, Fast, Instant, Immediately
**Exclusive:** Secret, Insider, Hidden, Little-Known, Underground
**Value:** Free, Essential, Ultimate, Complete, Definitive
**Emotion:** Amazing, Incredible, Surprising, Shocking, Unexpected
**Ease:** Simple, Easy, Effortless, Beginner, Step-by-Step
**Results:** Proven, Guaranteed, Works, Effective, Powerful

---

## Headline Testing

### A/B Test When Possible

If your platform supports it, test variations:
- Different numbers (7 vs 11)
- Question vs statement
- With/without power words
- Different benefits emphasized

### The "Would I Click This?" Test

Before publishing, ask:
1. Is it specific enough?
2. Does it promise clear value?
3. Does the article deliver on the promise?
4. Would I click this if I saw it in search results?

### Check Length

- **Search results:** 50-60 characters before truncation
- **Social shares:** Can be longer, but front-load important words
- **Email subject lines:** 40-50 characters for mobile

---

## What to Avoid

### Clickbait Patterns
- "You Won't Believe..." (yes I will)
- "This One Trick..." (scammy)
- "Shocking!" without actual shock
- Outrage bait that doesn't add value

### Vague Headlines
- ❌ "Some Thoughts on Productivity"
- ✅ "5 Productivity Systems I've Actually Used"

### Keyword Stuffing
- ❌ "Best Coffee Makers: Coffee Maker Reviews and Coffee Maker Buying Guide"
- ✅ "The 7 Best Coffee Makers for Every Budget (2024)"

### Misleading Claims
- Don't promise what you can't deliver
- Don't exaggerate results
- Don't imply false urgency

---

## Headline Generation Process

1. **Write 10+ variations** — Quantity leads to quality
2. **Mix formulas** — Try numbers, how-to, questions
3. **Include keyword** — For SEO
4. **Check length** — Trim if needed
5. **Read aloud** — Does it sound natural?
6. **Pick top 3** — Get feedback or A/B test
7. **Choose winner** — Go with gut if no data
